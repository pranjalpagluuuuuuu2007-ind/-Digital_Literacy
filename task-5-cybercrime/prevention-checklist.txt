🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Student’s Guide to Digital Security

The Golden UPI Rule: Seriously—never, ever enter your UPI PIN just to receive money. If someone tells you to type it to get paid, it’s a scam, no exceptions.

QR Code Caution: Don’t scan QR codes sent by strangers on WhatsApp, OLX, or social media. If you didn’t generate it yourself, don’t touch it—it’s usually a trick to empty your account.

Level Up Your Passwords: Stop reusing the same password everywhere. Make strong, unique passwords for your university portal, email, and other key accounts. Think of it as your first line of defense.

Lock it with 2FA: Turn on Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for Google, Instagram, LinkedIn—anywhere you can. That extra approval step on your phone makes it much harder for hackers to sneak in.

Double-Check the URL: Before typing login info, glance at the address bar. Make sure it starts with https:// and that the spelling is correct. One letter off can mean a fake site.

Skip Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Campus or cafe Wi-Fi is great for browsing, but don’t log into banking or UPI apps over it. Use mobile data for anything involving money.

Clean Up App Permissions: Check which apps can access your camera or gallery. If a calculator app suddenly wants access to your photos—revoke it. Seriously.

Ignore “Too Good to Be True” Links: “High-Paying Job” or “Free iPhone” messages? Always scams. If you didn’t apply or enter a contest, delete the message immediately.

🚨 If you do get scammed, act fast:

Report it: Go to the National Cyber Crime Portal.
Call for help: Dial 1930—especially for UPI or banking fraud.

Pro-Tip: Just pausing for a second before you click or scan can save you a world of trouble. Stay alert, think twice, and keep your digital life safe!
